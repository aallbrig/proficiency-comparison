Purpose

The Educational Stats Comparison project provides an interactive platform for analyzing and comparing educational outcomes across different birth year cohorts in the United States.

By mapping birth years to age-appropriate educational data points, we can track how different generations have progressed through the education system and compare their outcomes at similar life stages.

Why This Matters

Understanding educational trends across cohorts helps us:

  • Identify systemic changes: See how policy changes and reforms have impacted different generations
  • Compare outcomes fairly: Evaluate cohorts at comparable life stages rather than across different time periods
  • Inform future policy: Use historical data to predict and improve outcomes for current and future students
  • Understand inequality: Track how educational gaps have evolved over time

The Data

We aggregate data from multiple authoritative sources:

Literacy Rates

World Bank / UNESCO (1970-present)

Adult and youth literacy rates measuring basic reading comprehension.

Educational Attainment

US Census Bureau (1940-present)

Percentage of population with high school diplomas, bachelor's degrees, and advanced degrees.

Graduation Rates

NCES (1869-present)

High school graduation and dropout rates.

Test Proficiency

NAEP (1969-present)

Standardized test scores in reading, math, science, and writing.

Enrollment Rates

NCES (historical)

School enrollment by level and demographic group.

Early Childhood Metrics

NCES ECLS (1998-present)

Early literacy, school readiness, and developmental indicators.

Methodology

Cohort Mapping

Birth years are mapped to age-appropriate data:

  • Adult literacy measured at age 15+
  • High school graduation rates at age 18
  • Educational attainment at age 25+
  • Grade-level proficiency tests at ages 9, 13, and 17

Handling Recent Cohorts

For recent birth years (e.g., 2016), we use proxy data from current grade-level assessments to provide estimates of ongoing educational experiences.

Technology

This project is built with:

  • Go CLI: Data download and management tool
  • SQLite: Local data storage
  • Hugo: Static site generator
  • Bootstrap: Responsive UI framework
  • Chart.js: Data visualizations
